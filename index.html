Camel
  • Scientific name : Camelus.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • It is known as "The Ship of Desert" because it can comfortably move across sand due to its padded feet.

Chameleon
  • Scientific name : Chamaeleonidae.
  • Almost half of all known species live in Madagascar.
  • They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.

Flying-cicada
  • Scientific name : Cicadoidea.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound is a chorus made by an all-male cicada. (Females make a much quieter sound by flicking their wings)
  • Most have red-colored eyes. But occasionally they have blue, white or grayish eyes.

Panda
  • Scientific name : Ailuropoda melanoleuca.
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • An adult panda can eat 12-38 kilos of bamboo and can poop 28 kilos per day!

Pigeon
  • Scientific name : Columbidae.
  • They are intelligent enough to understand space and time.
  • They can find their way back to the nest from 1300 miles away.

Lion
  • Scientific name : Panthera leo.
  • A good gauge of a male's lion age is the darkness of his mane. The darker the mane, the older the lion.
  • A lion's roar can be heard from as far as 5 miles away.

T-rex
  • Scientific name : Tyrannosaurus rex.
  • Its scientific name comes from greek and latin words meaning 'Tyrant Lizard King'.
  • More than 20 aalmost-complete t rex skeletons have been found. The most perfect, nicknamed Sue, was unearthed in South Dakota, USA, 20 years ago, and was sold to a museum in Chicago for around 5 million pounds in 1997.


Penguin
  • Scientific name : Spheniscidae.
  • While other birds have wings for flying, penguins have adapted flippers to help them swim in the water.
  • The Emperor Penguin is the tallest of all penguin species, reaching as tall as 120 cm (47 in) in height.

Whale
  • Scientific name : Cetacea.
  • Whales are the largest animals to have ever lived - even larger than any dinosaur!
  • Gray whales make one of the longest annual migrations of any mammals. They travel about 10,000 miles round trip!
